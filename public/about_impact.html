Our Mission

imPacT the physical therapy industry, specifically Home Exercise Programs (HEP), in a way that channels patients’
frustration of “why me” into goals that push them to progress in their rehabilitation.

A story of Impact

Our application aims to increase the adherence rate of home exercise programs, while decreasing the time that clinicians have to spend re-printing and re-writing exercises. Additionally, the application plans to re-enforce and increase the success rate of full rehabilitation through customizable reminders for each exercise. The application will also serve as a motivator to patients through the progress tracker by allowing patients to set measurable goals throughout the process. Lastly, the application will work with the clinic to simplify scheduling and eliminate phone tag between parents and receptionists.

Our Founder and CEO, Kayla Armstrong, believes everything happens for a reason, but determining the reason for negative aspects in life is more challenging. It was not until the sixth surgery from sports that she believed she found the reason for her injuries: imPacT. The surgery that sparked the creation of imPacT was when she was entering her senior year at the Kelley School of Business at Indiana University. This caused her to join an entrepreneurship group on campus and soak up as much knowledge and resources that she could from the University. The following year she was able to make more progress and refine her business as a Master of Accounting student at the Kelley School of Business. Throughout the process, she utilized her resources in the professional sports industry speaking with team doctors, physical therapists, and St. Louis Blues Head Athletic Trainers. As well as conducting market research on athletes of all levels: high school, collegiate, and professional on their physical therapy experience and pain points.
